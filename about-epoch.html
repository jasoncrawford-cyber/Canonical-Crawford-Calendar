Epoch Definition & Justification

Home Calendar

Chosen Epoch

MLC Year 1, Month 1, Day 1 is defined as:

Gregorian date: 20 March 2000 (UTC)
Astronomical event: March Equinox

Rationale

  • Astronomical neutrality: The equinox is a physically defined, culture-independent event.
  • Seasonal symmetry: Month structure centers solstices and equinoxes at mid-months.
  • Millennial boundary: The year 2000 is a natural civil reference point.
  • No historical back-projection: Avoids retroactive calendar distortion.

Why Not a Historical Epoch?

The Millennial Lunisolar Calendar is a fixed astronomical system, not a reconstruction. Using a modern equinox avoids:

  • Uncertain ancient observations
  • Calendar reforms & political bias
  • Long-term seasonal drift

Stability Guarantee

Because the MLC is mathematically closed over a 334-year cycle, this epoch will never drift relative to the seasons.
